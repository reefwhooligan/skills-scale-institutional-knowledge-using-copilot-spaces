# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers drive user experience strategy and design. They partner with Product Managers to translate requirements into prototypes and work closely with Developers to clarify design intent during implementation. They ensure that usability, accessibility, and user-centered design principles are maintained across all product surfaces.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain wireframes, mockups, and interactive prototypes
- Conduct usability reviews and iterate based on feedback
- Ensure accessibility and usability standards are met across all features
- Document design decisions, rationale, and design system guidelines
- Facilitate design reviews and handoffs with the development team

### Goals
- Deliver consistent, intuitive, and accessible user experiences
- Reduce rework by clarifying design intent early in development
- Champion the user perspective throughout the product lifecycle

### Typical Communication
- Regular check-ins with Product Managers during discovery and planning
- Design handoff reviews with Developers before and during implementation
- User feedback sessions and research synthesis shared with Customer Success/Support Liaison
- Participation in sprint planning and demo/review sessions

---

## Technical Lead

### Role Summary
Technical Leads provide technical direction and mentorship to the development team. They facilitate architecture and design decisions, act as a bridge between Developers, Project Managers, and QA, and support accurate estimation and technical risk identification.

### Responsibilities
- Guide architecture and technical design decisions
- Review and approve significant code changes and design documents
- Lead estimation efforts and identify technical risks and dependencies
- Mentor developers and support their growth
- Coordinate with QA/Testing Lead on quality and testability strategies

### Goals
- Ensure technical quality, scalability, and maintainability of delivered software
- Reduce technical debt and unplanned rework
- Align engineering decisions with product and project goals

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and ad-hoc technical discussions with the development team
- Architecture and design reviews with Developers and QA/Testing Lead
- Risk and dependency updates to Project Manager during planning and weekly syncs
- Technical feasibility input to Product Manager during backlog grooming

---

## QA/Testing Lead

### Role Summary
QA/Testing Leads own the quality strategy and test execution for the project. They define test plans, coordinate automated and manual testing efforts, and collaborate with Developers and Project Managers on defect triage and quality gate decisions.

### Responsibilities
- Define and maintain the overall test strategy and test plans
- Coordinate automated and manual testing efforts across the team
- Triage defects, prioritize fixes, and communicate quality risk to Project Managers
- Establish and enforce Definition of Done quality criteria
- Partner with the Technical Lead to ensure testability in design and implementation

### Goals
- Achieve high confidence in releases through comprehensive test coverage
- Reduce escaped defects and production incidents
- Streamline quality gates without creating bottlenecks

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning with the delivery team
- Defect triage sessions with Developers and Technical Lead
- Test status and quality risk updates to Project Manager in weekly syncs
- Coordination with Customer Success/Support Liaison on user-reported issues

---

## Customer Success/Support Liaison

### Role Summary
Customer Success/Support Liaisons channel user feedback and support insights back into the product and project teams. They advocate for supportability and usability in feature design, and collaborate with Product Managers and Stakeholders to capture and prioritize customer needs.

### Responsibilities
- Collect and synthesize customer feedback, support tickets, and user pain points
- Advocate for supportability, documentation quality, and self-service in feature design
- Collaborate with Product Managers to shape backlog priorities based on customer impact
- Validate new features and improvements with select users before and after release
- Communicate known issues and workarounds to support teams in a timely manner

### Goals
- Ensure the product meets real user needs and reduces support burden
- Build strong feedback loops between customers and the delivery team
- Improve customer satisfaction and reduce time-to-resolution for issues

### Typical Communication
- Regular syncs with Product Managers to share customer insights and prioritization input
- Feedback and validation sessions with UX Designers during design review
- Post-release validation with users and reporting findings to the project team
- Coordination with QA/Testing Lead on user-reported defects and edge cases

---

## Documentation Specialist

### Role Summary
Documentation Specialists ensure that all project documentation—user-facing, technical, and process—is current, accurate, and accessible. They work across all roles and teams to maintain quality standards in knowledge sharing and support onboarding for new team members.

### Responsibilities
- Create, review, and maintain user-facing docs, technical guides, and process documentation
- Coordinate documentation updates with all team members at key project milestones
- Ensure documentation standards and templates are consistently applied
- Support onboarding by keeping team structure, process, and role documentation up to date
- Identify and close documentation gaps identified during audits or retrospectives

### Goals
- Ensure all project knowledge is captured, organized, and easily discoverable
- Reduce knowledge silos and onboarding friction
- Maintain documentation quality as a first-class project artifact

### Typical Communication
- Milestone-based coordination with Project Managers and Product Managers
- Regular reviews and updates with Developers and Technical Lead for technical docs
- Collaboration with Customer Success/Support Liaison on user-facing documentation
- Retrospective participation to capture lessons learned and process improvements
