## Overview

The OctoAcme project management framework emphasizes clear structure, collaborative practices, and continuous improvement throughout the project lifecycle. At its core, OctoAcme organizes work into distinct phases—initiation, planning, execution, release, and retrospective. Each phase has defined objectives and deliverables, supported by templates and checklists that ensure consistency. Projects begin with a lightweight initiation process (project one-pager, stakeholder alignment, and risk identification). Planning follows with backlog creation, estimation, and definition of done, aiming to break down work into shippable increments. Execution leverages visual project boards, small pull requests with automated checks, and regular demos, ensuring progress and risks are transparent.

Roles and responsibilities are explicitly defined to foster ownership and cross-functional collaboration. Project Managers coordinate delivery schedules and risk management; Product Managers prioritize the roadmap and measure outcomes; Developers implement features and maintain quality; QA/testers validate acceptance criteria; Stakeholders provide input and approval. Clearly articulated roles and communication channels reduce ambiguity and maintain alignment across teams.

Communication is frequent, structured, and multi-layered: daily (or twice-weekly) standups, weekly syncs, and milestone-based updates keep information flowing and blockers visible. Status and incident templates standardize reporting, while risks escalate through defined paths. All status information and risk registers are kept in-repo for transparency and single-source-of-truth access.

Quality assurance is tightly woven into the process: automated and manual tests, security scans, and smoke tests are required for release. Risk management is documented and revisited throughout the project lifecycle, with mitigation plans reviewed weekly. After releases, teams conduct retrospectives to capture learnings and prioritize improvements, which are tracked as backlog items or issues. This commitment to measurement and iteration helps OctoAcme deliver reliable outcomes while enabling team growth and adaptability.
